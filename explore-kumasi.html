WHY NOT KUMASI

Invest In Kumasi

Kumasi is dubbed the Garden city of Africa and has an enviable rich history dating from the 16th century, way before the Europeans arrived on the Gulf of Guinea of the Atlantic Ocean.

Kumasi is the bedrock of rich culture preserved over 400 years and still relevant in the dispensation of modern chieftaincy and governance.

Kumasi currently has a modern international airport that connects to the rest of the world, making Kumasi an enviable option to invest heavily in real estate.

The region is blessed with various tourist attractions, the most recommended being the Manhyia palace, the centre of excellence for culture and respect for all things culture.

There is also the natural crater lake Bosomtwe, the monkey sanctuary as well as several well-preserved colonial buildings. The Ashanti region which has the city of Kumasi as its capital has burgeoning population of 5 million people mainly youth with a passion for technical and vocational skills.

The Ashanti region is well known to have the best artisans who support a very diverse economy from agriculture to tourism to manufacturing.

This makes the standard of living lower compared to other parts of Ghana and the West African sub-region. Kumasi is enviably positioned and connects to important cities in the West African sub region such as Lagos, Lomé, Ouagadougou, and Yamoussoukro.

The Land Management regime has minimal disputes as there is a strong and robust traditional system implemented. Land disputes are resolved along the lines of traditional authority and finality to all matters rest with the king of the Ashanti kingdom.

This makes access to land easy and there is a huge resource availability in the region as well.

From raw materials such as timber, gold, diamonds, granite, coarse sand aggregate to human capital.

Finally, to cap it all, Kumasi has one of the most serene landscapes and peaceful people in the whole of Ghana and the West African sub region.
